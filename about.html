About Us

Our name pays homage to the majestic rivers that converge here, their waters carrying stories of ancient migrations and modern adventures. Just as these waterways sustain the abundant wildlife of Chobe National Park, we sustain travelers with exceptional hospitality—spacious rooms with river views, gourmet dining featuring local flavors, and safari experiences that begin right at our doorstep. What truly sets Two Rivers apart is our privileged position between two worlds: the thrill of daily game drives where elephants roam freely, and the serenity of returning to your private balcony overlooking the rivers' eternal dance. Whether you're here for the legendary Chobe sunsets, Victoria Falls excursions, or simply to witness nature's grandeur, our team—proudly rooted in the local community—ensures every moment becomes part of your African story. This isn't just a hotel stay; it's a front-row seat to the wild heart of Southern Africa.

Beyond Our Doors

At Two Rivers Hotel, every detail is designed to immerse you in effortless luxury. Step into a sanctuary where modern elegance meets warm hospitality—from our art-filled lobby with its double-height windows to the tranquil river views that greet you each morning. Whether you're here to unwind beside our infinity pool, savor locally inspired cuisine at The Riverside Grill, or explore the vibrant culture just moments away, your experience begins the moment you arrive. Discover why guests return year after year to this hidden gem where relaxation and adventure flow together.

  • Riverside Dining

    Savor fresh catches from the Chobe and Zambezi rivers at our partner restaurants, where local flavors meet stunning water views. Try the signature bream or kapenta fish, paired with Botswana’s finest wines.

  • Waterborne Adventures

    Kayak, fish, or take a sunset cruise where the Chobe and Zambezi embrace—our concierge arranges private guides for these iconic experiences.

  • Confluence Wildlife

    Where two rivers meet, ecosystems thrive. Spot elephants bathing, rare bird species, and the famous Chobe lions on guided walks or sunset boat cruises from our private dock.
